# Writing Style Notes

Date: 2026-05-22

Purpose: keep paper-facing and public-facing text from drifting into overly promotional or obviously AI-generated prose.

## General Rule

Use a calm research voice. Prefer direct claims, concrete numbers, and clear boundaries over motivational framing.

## Avoid

- Emojis or decorative icons.
- Repeated encouragement such as "perfect", "great", "gold mine", or "now focus".
- Excessive bold text.
- Symmetric list structures when a paragraph would read more naturally.
- Formulaic openings such as "看到这个结果，我要说".
- Overclaiming from operational constructs to subjective experience.

## Prefer

- Plain statements of what was measured.
- Short paragraphs with natural transitions.
- Explicit claim boundaries.
- Negative results described as measurement evidence, not as failure or success theater.
- "This suggests..." only when the preceding result directly supports the inference.

## Example

Avoid:

> This is the real breakthrough: the system has agency without self.

Prefer:

> In 37.5% of tested conditions, independent agency was high while independent boundary scores remained low. This supports a conservative operational interpretation: predictive control can be strong in the Transformer-like substrate even when current boundary-self probes fail.

## Paper-Specific Boundary

The paper may claim validated operational convergence and mechanism sensitivity. It should not claim subjective consciousness, phenomenological selfhood, or human-equivalent agency.
